Frequently Asked Questions

What are the main advantages of pneumatic actuators?

Pneumatic actuators are simple, reliable, and provide fast response times with high force output using compressed air. They are well-suited for hazardous environments since they do not use electricity, making them intrinsically safe.

What is the difference between double acting and spring return actuators?

Double acting actuators use air pressure for both opening and closing the valve, providing precise control in both directions. Spring return actuators use air pressure to open and a spring mechanism to return the valve to its default position when air pressure is lost, providing fail-safe operation.

Are your pneumatic actuators suitable for hazardous areas?

Yes, we offer ATEX and IECEx certified pneumatic actuators designed specifically for safe operation in hazardous and explosive environments. Our actuators meet SIL3 safety standards and are PED compliant.

What torque range do you offer?

Our pneumatic actuators cover an extensive torque range from as low as 5 Nm (micro applications) up to 120,867 Nm (heavy-duty applications), ensuring we have a solution for virtually any valve automation requirement.
